## Purpose of This Document

This is the entry point to the CGP v0.4 document suite. It is deliberately short. It describes what CGP is, how the v0.4 documentation is structured, and where different readers should go next.

The previous single-document draft (v0.3) has been split into four documents of different lifecycles and audiences. The split exists because the protocol, its applications, and its institutional governance answer different questions, change at different rates, and benefit from being criticised independently of each other.

## What CGP Is, in One Paragraph

CGP is a framework for grassroots democratic governance that generates continuous, policy-level mandate through a triadic token system anchored to real-world fiat liabilities. Citizens deliberate on public policy in a distributed discussion substrate; politicians who deliver on ratified mandates are rewarded through an appreciating token whose value is bounded by the surplus generated by the very tax pool that underwrites the system. Three token classes — Citizen, Agent, and Poli — move in reciprocal relation modelled on Ohm's Law, with no bilateral exchange permitted. The structure is designed to be capture-resistant by mathematical construction rather than by policed enforcement.

---

## Why Now

A small number of AI companies and their principals are moving to concentrate unprecedented economic, scientific, and military decision-making power in the hands of a few dozen people. Existing representative democracy — a single binary vote every three or four years — cannot match the resolution or speed of decision-making that AI-empowered centralised actors are now capable of. The machinery of consent is too slow, too coarse, and too easily captured.

CGP is an attempt to build governance infrastructure sized for the challenge: continuous, policy-level, grassroots, cryptographically anchored, and structurally resistant to the capture modes that have disabled previous attempts.

A fuller treatment of the political-economic context is preserved in the Preface to v0.3, which this suite supersedes architecturally but does not attempt to improve rhetorically.

## The Document Suite

The v0.4 suite consists of four documents, each addressed to a different question.

**CGP Core Protocol** (`CGP_Core_Protocol_v0_4.md`) specifies the architecture: token mechanics, Proof of Deliberation and Proof of Ratification, the Ohm's Law relationship, the discussion substrate, the AI agent layer, corruption-resistance properties, and the technical stack. This is the citable, stable document — the equivalent of an RFC or a yellow paper. It is addressed to implementers, cryptoeconomists, and anyone whose purpose is to criticise or build the protocol itself.

**CGP Application Variants** (`CGP_Application_Variants_v0_4.md`) catalogues the domains in which CGP can be instantiated. The protocol is phylum-level; its applications are species adapted to their environments. State CGP (SCGP) anchors to tax pools and serves citizens. Corporate CGP (CCGP) anchors to profit pools and serves employees or shareholders. Non-profit CGP (NCGP) anchors to donations and serves donors and beneficiaries. Cooperative CGP (CoopCGP) anchors to member dues and serves cooperative members. Each variant inherits the phylum-level invariants but adapts anchor, stakeholder definition, performance metric, regulatory environment, and time horizon to its domain.

**CGP Governance** (`CGP_Governance_v0_4.md`) specifies the institutional body that stewards the protocol — its legal form, funding model, amendment process, relationship to forks, and bootstrap path toward eventual self-governance. The protocol itself is a specification that anyone may implement or fork under GPL v3. The governance body is a specific organisation that maintains the reference specification, licenses corporate deployments, and represents the project in regulatory conversations. Separating the two documents is a safety property: the protocol survives institutional failure because forks are legally free to carry it forward, and the governance body cannot unilaterally change the protocol without a visible amendment process that forks are free to reject.

The present document — this Overview — is the fourth. It exists to orient readers and to record the structure of the suite. It is expected to be short. If it grows significantly, that is a sign that content has escaped the other documents and should be migrated back.

## Variants at a Glance

The four application variants currently identified share all phylum-level invariants: triadic token structure with no bilateral trades, PoD and PoR as distinct consensus events, equal-share dividends for deliberation participants, emergent reciprocal supply against an anchor asset, transparency as a safety property, and free-speech discussion substrate.

They differ in the axes of anchor and stakeholder.

State CGP (SCGP) is the default reading for most of the suite. Tax pool as anchor. Citizens as stakeholders. Policy delivery as the performance metric. Within SCGP, municipal, state/county, and federal subspecies have materially different oracle problems and timescales — potholes are either filled or not, but foreign-policy outcomes are often disputed for decades.

Corporate CGP (CCGP) anchors to profit pool or performance-bonus reserve. Stakeholders are employees, shareholders, or a hybrid. Performance metrics are corporate KPIs that existing audit infrastructure already verifies. Regulatory path is short because corporations can adopt internal governance changes without legislative action. CCGP is the likely first field test at scale.

Non-profit CGP (NCGP) anchors to donations or grants. Stakeholders are donors, beneficiaries, or both. Performance metrics are mission-specific and often already machine-measurable. Small NPOs are candidates for an early field test of the fully autonomous AI/DAO Agent, because they cannot afford human tax-agent-equivalent intermediaries and their mission verification is often simpler than tax fiat handling.

Cooperative CGP (CoopCGP) anchors to member dues. Stakeholders are members. Performance metrics are cooperative outcomes as defined by the specific coop.

Details, token mappings, and cross-variant considerations live in the Application Variants document.

## Reading Paths

Different readers want different things. The suggested entry points are:

For someone new to CGP: read this Overview, then the Preface and first three sections of the Core Protocol. That is roughly thirty minutes and covers what the project is trying to do.

For a cryptoeconomist or protocol engineer: skim the Overview, then read the Core Protocol in full, with particular attention to Sections 4 (Token Triarchy), 5 (PoD and PoR), and 6 (Emergent Dynamics). Ignore the Variants and Governance documents until the protocol itself has been evaluated.

For a corporate adopter or NPO evaluating CCGP or NCGP: read the Overview, then the relevant variant section of Application Variants, then return to the Core Protocol for the mechanics. Governance is optional at this stage.

For a potential contributor to the governance body itself: all four documents, in the order Overview → Core Protocol → Application Variants → Governance.

For a critic or sceptic: the Core Protocol's Section 11 (Open Questions), which names what is genuinely unresolved and invites attack.

## Relationship to v0.3

The v0.3 single-document draft is superseded by this suite but remains a useful historical reference. Material changes from v0.3 are catalogued in the Core Protocol's revision note.

The substantive architectural changes since v0.3 are, in descending order of importance:

Proof of Ratification (PoR) has been named as a distinct consensus event from Proof of Deliberation (PoD). The two serve different functions and fire at different moments in the mandate lifecycle. v0.3 collapsed both under PoD.

Engineered inflation and deflation schedules have been removed from the design entirely. The reciprocal supply relationship between Citizen Coin and Poli Coin against the Agent Coin anchor produces emergent deflationary dynamics without any externally imposed curve. This simplifies the protocol meaningfully.

The dividend mechanism for citizen participants has been specified as equal-share among participants in a ratified mandate, independent of bid size. Bid size serves as an intensity signal for mandate prioritisation; democratic reward is equal. This decouples wealth from governance reward while preserving preference expression.

The token ratio — Poli price relative to Citizen price — has been identified as an emergent accountability signal readable in real time by any observer. High Poli-to-Citizen ratio indicates politicians delivering faster than citizens are generating mandates; low ratio indicates the inverse. This is a property worth naming explicitly.

The application-variant structure has been introduced. v0.3 implicitly assumed State CGP and did not distinguish variants. v0.4 treats the four variants as peers within a common protocol phylum.

Governance of the protocol as an institutional concern has been separated from the protocol specification itself.
